Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 21, 2026

Welcome to Shared Living. We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal data. This privacy policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your information when you use our mobile application.

1. Information We Collect

We collect information to provide a better experience for managing tasks, chores, groceries, and expenses within your household:

  • Account Information: When you register an account, we collect your email address, unique username, and phone number. This information is secured and managed via Firebase Authentication.
  • Household Data: All task logs, chore rosters, expense balances, grocery shopping lists, and live chat messages that you submit to the application are stored securely in our Firebase Firestore database.
  • Device Push Tokens: With your permission, we collect and route Expo Push Notification tokens to notify you about chore assignments, reminders, payments, and messaging alerts.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information for the following purposes:

  • To set up and manage your user profile and household connections.
  • To sync real-time bills, tasks, and chat notifications between you and your roommates.
  • To schedule push reminders for chores and notify you of modifications to shared balances.
  • To collect crashes and analytics logs (via Sentry) to diagnose bug fixes and optimize performance.

3. Data Retention and Deletion

We hold your data only as long as you keep an active household or user profile. We implement a strict data retention policy where historical subcollection data is backed up and purged periodically:

  • Archived Logs: Chore, grocery, and expense listings older than three months are archived and kept for up to five months, after which they are permanently removed.
  • Account Deletion: You have the absolute right to delete your account at any time. When you initiate account deletion from the "Legal & Compliance" tab in settings:
    • Your email and registration identifiers are permanently removed from Firebase Authentication.
    • Your Firestore user profile is destroyed.
    • Your username mapping is deleted to free it up for reuse.
    • Your UID is automatically removed from your household members array.

4. Security

We implement strict Firestore database security rules that isolate subcollection access. Only authenticated users who belong to a specific household's verified member list can read or modify that household's tasks, expenses, groceries, and messages.
